# BrightMotors
# Introduction
This project analyses a large dataset of car sales transactions to uncover trends, patterns, and actionable insights. The goal is to help automotive businesses optimize pricing strategies, inventory planning, and marketing efforts.

# Problem Statement
Car dealerships often struggle to identify which vehicle makes, models, and regions drive the most revenue. Additionally, factors such as mileage and model year significantly impact pricing, but these relationships are not always clear. This project addresses these challenges by leveraging data analytics to provide evidence-based recommendations.

# Objectives
- Analyze historical car sales data to identify top-performing makes, models, and regions.
- Understand the relationship between mileage and selling price.
- Detect trends in sales over time and across regions.
- Provide actionable recommendations for pricing, inventory, and marketing strategies.


# Tools Used
- MSSQL (for data transformation and querying)
- Excel (Pivot tables and charts)
- Google Looker Studio (optional for dashboard visualization)


# Key Insights
- Total Records: 558,825 transactions
- Average Price: $13,611 | Average Mileage: ~68,321 miles
- Top Makes: Ford, Chevrolet, Nissan, Toyota, Dodge
- Top Models: Altima, F-150, Fusion, Camry, Escape
- Top Regions: Florida, California, Pennsylvania, Texas, Georgia
- Mileage vs Price Correlation: -0.58 (strong negative correlation)

# Recommendations
- Marketing Focus: Prioritize top-selling makes and models in campaigns.
- Regional Strategy: Target high-performing regions with localized promotions.
- Pricing Strategy: Adjust pricing for older models to clear inventory.
- Highlight Low Mileage: Emphasize low-mileage vehicles in premium listings.
- Inventory Planning: Stock popular models to meet demand.
- Seasonal Promotions: Align campaigns with historical peak sales periods.
